## Tweet 2
Of 433 companies, here's how they split by what they actually build:

- Applications: 167 (39%)
- Models: 92 (21%)
- Automation: 74 (17%)
- Data: 60 (14%)
- Infrastructure: 40 (9%)

Most AI x Finance money is in apps. The infrastructure layer is severely undercapitalized relative to its strategic importance.

## Tweet 3
200 of 433 companies use predictive ML. Only 35 are classified as agentic.

Agentic AI is the current hype cycle. But in finance, the boring old ML — fraud models, credit scoring, churn prediction — is where the revenue actually is.

The hype map and the revenue map are not the same map.

## Tweet 4
45 of the 433 companies have been acquired. That's a 10% acquisition rate — unusually high for a sector people describe as "early."

Finance incumbents (banks, insurers, exchanges) are buying rather than building. That's not a sign of a nascent market. It's a sign of a mature one that's being quietly restructured.
